## 本地摘录 / 结构化正文

By PEO C3N Public Communications Directorate, September 2, 2025.

The U.S. Army awarded a new OTA agreement to Lockheed Martin Rotary and Mission Systems and their team of non-traditional innovators and commercial technology providers. The agreement aims to advance NGC2 division-level prototyping and experimentation by delivering an integrated data layer capability to the 25th Infantry Division.

The OTA was awarded through the NGC2 competitive Commercial Solutions Offering on behalf of PEO C3N for $26 million with a not-to-exceed period of performance of 16 months.

The new OTA builds on the Army’s recently extended NGC2 OTA agreement to Team Anduril. In July, the Army continued an OTA agreement to Team Anduril to maintain NGC2 momentum from Project Convergence experimentation. That agreement will deliver a NGC2 prototype architecture to the 4th Infantry Division, which is conducting prototyping across applications, data, infrastructure, and transport within a full “technology stack.”

Lessons learned by the 4th Infantry Division and 25th Infantry Division will provide insights into how heavy and light units will apply NGC2 differently. These efforts will examine how NGC2 elements function across the technology stack, with a focus on the data layer’s ability to ingest, transport, and organize data from multiple warfighting systems.
