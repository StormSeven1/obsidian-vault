## 本地摘录 / 结构化正文

By COL Tyler D. Olsen, January 29, 2026.

NGC2 aims to integrate data, artificial intelligence, and resilient communications into a single decision-support framework. For logistics commanders, this means moving from fragmented, delayed unit-based reporting to a common operating picture that is timely, accurate, and actionable.

NGC2 is built on four foundational layers: application, data, infrastructure, and transport.

Application layer provides dashboards and decision-support tools that transform raw data into predictive analytics and sustainment recommendations.

Data layer integrates sensor and platform inputs into a unified operational picture, using AI and ML to filter, validate, and prioritize data streams.

Infrastructure layer anchors computing, storage, and user devices at the tactical edge. Transport layer ensures secure and reliable movement of data even under cyberattack, EW, or DDIL conditions.

At Ivy Sting 1 in September 2025, AXS demonstrated how all four layers could be integrated into a single division-level C2 environment. The article states that 4ID showed how data could flow seamlessly across the formation, and that fires was among the first warfighting functions to adopt these capabilities.

Future events are expected to incorporate AI/ML, autonomous resupply orchestration, cognitive decision support that simulates outcomes, and edge analytics capable of operating in disconnected environments.
