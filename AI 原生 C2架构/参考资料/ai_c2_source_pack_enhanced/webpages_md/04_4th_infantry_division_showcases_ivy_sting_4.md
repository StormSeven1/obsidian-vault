By U.S. Army Public Affairs, February 9, 2026.

On Feb. 6, the 4th Infantry Division at Fort Carson, Colorado, completed Ivy Sting 4, a significant demonstration of the Army's Next Generation Command and Control program.

Joe Welch emphasized rapid prototyping and integration of commercial technologies, highlighting a commercial-first strategy that allows for quicker fielding and technology refresh cycles.

Maj. Gen. Pat Ellis said Sting 4 was the largest exercise to date and represented exponential growth from initial events. The exercise focused not only on command and control but also on airspace management and medical logistics.

Soldier feedback from these exercises is described as invaluable in shaping future technology.
